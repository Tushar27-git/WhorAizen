## The core creative brief, in your own words (don't skip this)
We want the cinematic, moody, painterly atmosphere of Complementary Shaders and its well-known horror-themed edit "Spooklementary" — soft directional light, thick believable fog, dramatic god rays, gorgeous sunsets, storm-reactive weather — but rebalanced so that nights, caves, and especially thunderstorms never collapse into near-black. Spooklementary intentionally crushes the black level and adds horror-driven flicker/eyes/static/blood-moon gimmicks for a scary effect; we want the opposite emotional target using the *same rendering vocabulary*: a little brighter, atmospheric rather than oppressive, cinematic rather than horror. `DESIGN.md` §2-3 spells out exactly what to keep and what to cut from that lineage — internalize it, it's the taste bar every phase gets checked against.
